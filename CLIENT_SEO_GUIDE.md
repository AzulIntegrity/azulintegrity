## ‚úÖ SEO Writing Best Practices

### **Page Titles**
- Keep under 60 characters (Google cuts off longer titles)
- Include your business name
- Put most important keywords first
- Make it click-worthy but professional

**Good Example**: `"Virtual CPA Services | Tax & Bookkeeping | Azul Integrity"`
**Too Long**: `"Professional Virtual CPA Services for Small Businesses Including Tax Preparation, Bookkeeping, Payroll Processing and Financial Consulting | Azul Integrity Accounting"`

### **Descriptions**
- 150-160 characters is the sweet spot
- Include a call-to-action
- Mention your location coverage (nationwide, virtual)
- Be specific about benefits

**Good Example**: `"Expert virtual CPA services for small businesses nationwide. Professional bookkeeping, tax prep, and financial consulting. Licensed CPA with 25+ years experience."`

### **Keywords**
- Use phrases people actually search for
- Include location terms if relevant (`"virtual"`, `"remote"`, `"nationwide"`)
- Mix broad and specific terms
- Check what your competitors use
- Think like your customers

## üìä When to Update Your SEO

### **Monthly Reviews** (Recommended)
- Check which keywords are working
- Update based on current business focus
- Adjust for seasonal services

### **Update Triggers**
- **New Services**: Add relevant keywords when you offer something new
- **Seasonal Changes**: Emphasize tax prep in tax season, year-end services in December
- **Market Changes**: If you notice competitors targeting new keywords
- **Business Growth**: As you expand services or target new clients

### **What NOT to Change**
- Your core protected keywords (system handles these automatically)
- Working titles and descriptions unless you have a specific reason
- Keywords that are already performing well

## üö® Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Keyword Stuffing**: Don't repeat the same keyword multiple times
2. **Too Generic**: `"services"` is less effective than `"payroll services"`
3. **Too Specific**: `"QuickBooks 2024 setup for restaurants"` might be too narrow
4. **Competitor Names**: Don't use other CPA firm names
5. **Irrelevant Terms**: Stick to accounting and business-related keywords
